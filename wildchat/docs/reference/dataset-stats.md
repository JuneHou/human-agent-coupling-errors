## Notes, reconciliation, and coupling-relevant takeaways

- **ShareChat count vs. the paper.** Paper reports 142,808 conversations / 660,293 turns. We
  observe **129,584** conversations in the language-filtered release; the `filtered_out` JSON
  removes **8,842** non-target-language conversations (chatgpt 7,345; grok 621; perplexity 335;
  gemini 522; claude 19). 129,584 + 8,842 = 138,426, leaving a ~3% (4,382) residual vs. 142,808
  (likely additional dedup/processing upstream). Our "messages" (1,199,899) ≈ 2× the paper's
  "turns" because a turn = one user+`llm` round.
- **Thinking traces are effectively Claude-only.** Claude: 28.6% of `llm` messages carry a
  non-empty `thinking` field, but only **911 conversations**. Grok has the column but it's filled
  ~0.3% of the time; ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini have no thinking column. ⇒ The AI-internal-state
  vs. surfaced-text probe (AI→Human axis) is a **small, Claude-centric sample**, fine for
  qualitative reading but not large-scale statistics. **NB:** the `thinking` field is a python-repr
  dict that mixes **genuine (summarized) reasoning** with **tool / web-search Request/Response dumps**;
  the genuine-reasoning subset is **1,008 turns / 254 convs** (see `../shared-findings.md`). The
  earlier "reasons 2× what it surfaces" length stat was an artifact of the tool-JSON blobs.
- **Predecessor invisible-failure rate validated.** On the 100K condition-C / opus annotations:
  failure_visibility = invisible 49,368 · none 37,443 · visible 7,632 · mixed 5,557. Among the
  62,557 *failures*, invisible = **78.9% ≈ the paper's "79% invisible."** Confirms our reading of
  the annotation schema and the conversation_id join.
- **Era / model gap.** WildChat is 2023-04 → 2024-04, all GPT-3.5/GPT-4 (2023-era). ShareChat is
  newer and multi-platform (e.g. Grok 2025, Gemini 2.5 Flash mid-2025) — so a WildChat↔ShareChat
  comparison conflates *platform* and *time period* (a confound to flag in Step 2).
- **Selection bias.** WildChat is unsolicited/unfiltered; ShareChat is post-hoc *shared* URLs.
  ShareChat length is **bimodal**: a large single-turn mass (41–62% by platform) *and* a heavier
  multi-turn tail than WildChat (mean turns 3.8–5.3 vs 2.34; max 851/1337). Shared sessions skew
  toward quick showcases or substantive long sessions — a publish-what-impresses filter that
  threatens the visible/invisible failure ratio, so ShareChat is not a safe basis for failure-rate
  comparison. (See `wildchat-vs-sharechat.md`.)
